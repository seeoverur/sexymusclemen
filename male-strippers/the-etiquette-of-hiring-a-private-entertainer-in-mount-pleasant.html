The Etiquette of Hiring a Private Entertainer

A Complete Guide to Professional Standards & Mutual Respect

What to expect, how to prepare, and creating the best experience for everyone

Hiring a private entertainer for your Mount Pleasant celebration is an exciting decision that can elevate your party from ordinary to extraordinary. Whether it's a bachelorette party, birthday celebration, or special event, professional entertainment creates memories that last far beyond the event itself.

However, many first-time clients feel uncertain about what to expect, how to prepare, and what constitutes appropriate etiquette when working with private performers. This guide answers those questions, helping you create a comfortable, respectful, and enjoyable experience for everyone involved—your guests, the performer, and yourself.

Understanding professional entertainment etiquette isn't just about following rules—it's about building mutual respect between client and performer, ensuring everyone feels comfortable, and creating the optimal environment for an unforgettable performance. When everyone understands expectations and boundaries, the experience becomes more relaxed, more fun, and more memorable.

Why This Guide Matters

Professional entertainers in Mount Pleasant, like those from Sexy Muscle Men, appreciate working with informed, respectful clients. When you understand the etiquette and expectations, you signal that you value professionalism, which in turn helps the performer deliver their best work. This guide ensures you get the premium experience you're paying for while treating the entertainer with the respect they deserve.

Before the Performance: Booking & Preparation

1Initial Contact & Booking

Be Clear and Professional

When reaching out to book an entertainer, provide clear information about your event:

  • Date, time, and location of your event
  • Type of celebration (bachelorette party, birthday, etc.)
  • Approximate number of guests
  • Any special requests or themes
  • Venue type (private home, hotel suite, event space)
Professional Standard: Treat booking communication like any professional service. Clear, respectful communication helps the entertainer prepare appropriately and ensures your expectations are met.
Ask Questions Upfront

Don't be shy about asking questions! Professional entertainers expect and welcome questions such as:

  • "What's included in the performance?"
  • "How much space do you need?"
  • "What should we do to prepare?"
  • "Can we customize any aspects of the show?"
  • "What are your payment terms?"
  • "What's your cancellation policy?"
Understand Pricing

Professional entertainment has set pricing based on experience, performance duration, travel, and other factors. The quoted price typically includes:

  • The performance itself
  • Travel to your location
  • Professional costume(s)
  • Music and sound equipment
  • Professional liability insurance
✓ DO:
  • Book as early as possible, especially for weekend dates
  • Provide accurate information about your event
  • Ask about payment methods and deposit requirements
  • Get details in writing or via email
  • Share any special venue requirements (parking, access codes, etc.)
✗ DON'T:
  • Negotiate excessively on price—professionals set fair rates
  • Book last-minute and expect immediate availability
  • Withhold important information about your venue or event
  • Make inappropriate requests or comments
  • Assume services beyond what was discussed are included

2Preparing Your Space

Creating an appropriate performance space shows respect for the entertainer's work and helps ensure a great show.

Space Requirements
  • Clear area: Approximately 8'x8' minimum for the performance
  • Seating arranged: Create a semi-circle or theater-style seating facing the performance area
  • Furniture moved: Remove coffee tables or fragile items from the performance zone
  • Good lighting: Ensure the space is well-lit (performers often bring their own accent lighting)
  • Music capability: Confirm your sound system works, or rely on the performer's equipment
Privacy Considerations
  • Close blinds or curtains if you have windows facing neighbors
  • Lock doors or designate someone to manage access
  • Ensure pets are secured in another room
  • Have a private area available if the performer needs to change or prepare
Professional Standard: Think of preparing your space like setting up for any professional service. You wouldn't hire a caterer without clearing counter space, and you shouldn't hire an entertainer without preparing an appropriate performance area.

3Preparing Your Guests

Brief your guests beforehand to ensure everyone understands expectations and boundaries.

Set the Tone

Before the entertainer arrives, have a quick conversation with your group:

  • "We have professional entertainment coming—let's all be respectful"
  • "The performer will engage with [guest of honor], but let them lead the interaction"
  • "Photos and videos are okay, but we need to respect the performer's boundaries"
  • "This is meant to be fun for everyone, including the entertainer"
Manage Expectations

Help your guests understand what to expect:

  • Performance duration (typically 20-45 minutes)
  • The show is interactive but professional
  • The entertainer is working, not a party guest
  • Everyone should participate in creating a fun, supportive atmosphere
✓ DO Encourage:
  • Cheering, clapping, and supportive enthusiasm
  • Following the performer's cues and prompts
  • Taking photos and videos (within reason)
  • Laughter and enjoyment
  • Respectful participation when invited

During the Performance: Boundaries & Respect

4Understanding Consent & Boundaries

The most important aspect of working with professional entertainers is understanding and respecting consent and boundaries. This creates a comfortable environment for everyone.

The Golden Rule

The performer controls the interaction. They decide what happens, when it happens, and who participates. Guests should never initiate physical contact, make demands, or overstep boundaries the performer hasn't explicitly invited.

What Professional Performers Offer
  • Choreographed performance: Professional dance routines and showmanship
  • Audience interaction: Engaging directly with the guest of honor and group
  • Photo opportunities: Posing for pictures with guests after or during the show
  • Customized moments: Special touches based on what you discussed when booking
  • Fun, energetic atmosphere: Creating excitement and memorable moments
Respecting Physical Boundaries

Professional entertainers have clear boundaries about physical contact:

✓ APPROPRIATE:
  • Following the performer's lead when they initiate interaction
  • Accepting when they invite you to participate in a specific way
  • Maintaining the boundaries they establish
  • Asking permission before taking photos with them
  • Staying in your seat unless invited to participate
✗ NEVER APPROPRIATE:
  • Grabbing, groping, or uninvited touching of any kind
  • Attempting to remove clothing beyond what the performer removes
  • Making sexually explicit comments or demands
  • Trying to pull the performer off-course from their routine
  • Aggressive or forceful behavior
  • Expecting anything beyond professional entertainment
Professional Standard: Treat the entertainer the way you'd want to be treated in their position. They're providing a service you hired them for—respect their professional boundaries just as you would with any other professional (a massage therapist, personal trainer, etc.).

5Photography & Social Media

Taking Photos and Videos

Most professional entertainers allow and even encourage photos and videos, as they're part of creating lasting memories. However, there are etiquette guidelines:

✓ DO:
  • Ask the performer at the start if photos/videos are okay
  • Designate 1-2 people as primary photographers so everyone else can enjoy
  • Take photos during appropriate moments, not disrupting the flow
  • Get group photos with the performer after the show
  • Focus on capturing your group's reactions and fun
✗ DON'T:
  • Use flash photography that interferes with the performance
  • Get so close with your phone that you're in the way
  • Post content to social media that identifies the performer without permission
  • Share photos/videos for commercial purposes
  • Film the entire performance in a way that could be redistributed
Social Media Considerations

When posting about your experience:

  • Photos of your group having fun? Absolutely!
  • Short clips showing the energy and excitement? Great!
  • Full performance videos? Generally not appropriate without explicit permission
  • Tagging or identifying the performer? Ask first—some prefer privacy
Professional Standard: Think about the performer's livelihood. They make their living from booking performances. Sharing your joy and excitement helps their business; sharing full performance videos could harm it by giving away what people pay for.

6Managing the Guest of Honor

If your event centers on a guest of honor (bride-to-be, birthday person), help ensure they're comfortable and having fun:

Reading the Room
  • Watch the guest of honor's reactions—are they genuinely enjoying themselves?
  • If they seem overwhelmed, signal the performer or step in supportively
  • Encourage but don't force participation
  • Make sure their comfort is prioritized
Creating the Right Energy
  • Your enthusiasm sets the tone for the room
  • Cheer, clap, and show support—it makes everyone more comfortable
  • Help create a fun, judgment-free atmosphere
  • Remind everyone this is about celebrating, not embarrassing
What Professional Entertainers Appreciate

Experienced performers can read a room and adjust their performance accordingly. They appreciate when:

  • The group is enthusiastic and engaged
  • Everyone respects boundaries
  • The guest of honor is genuinely enjoying themselves
  • The energy is fun and supportive, not aggressive or uncomfortable
  • Hosts help manage any guests who might overstep

After the Performance: Tipping & Appreciation

7Tipping Etiquette

Tipping is customary and expected for private entertainers, separate from their performance fee. Think of it like tipping a wedding DJ, bartender, or any service professional.

Standard Tipping Guidelines
Industry Standard: 15-20% of the performance fee

For exceptional service, enthusiasm, or if the performer went above and beyond, 25-30% is appropriate and greatly appreciated.

Calculating Tips

Examples based on common performance fees:

  • $300 performance → $45-$60 tip (15-20%)
  • $400 performance → $60-$80 tip (15-20%)
  • $500 performance → $75-$100 tip (15-20%)
How to Tip
  • Cash is preferred: Most entertainers prefer cash tips
  • Discreet handoff: Give the tip at the end, thanking them for their performance
  • Group tips: For large groups, collect $5-10 from each guest
  • Envelope method: Put cash in an envelope with a thank-you note
✓ Appropriate Tip Scenarios:
  • Performer arrived on time and fully prepared
  • Performance met or exceeded expectations
  • Entertainer was professional and courteous
  • They adapted well to your group's energy
  • Standard 15-20% is always appropriate for good service
✓ Generous Tip Scenarios:
  • Performer went significantly above and beyond
  • They accommodated special requests beautifully
  • The performance was exceptional and memorable
  • They handled a difficult situation with grace
  • They stayed longer or added extra touches

Why Tipping Matters

Entertainment professionals invest significantly in their careers—fitness training, choreography development, costumes, equipment, insurance, and maintaining their brand. Tips acknowledge this investment and the work they put into making your event special. Good tippers build reputations in the entertainment community, which can result in priority booking and special considerations for future events.

8Showing Appreciation Beyond Tips

Reviews and Referrals

Professional entertainers rely on word-of-mouth and reviews. You can show appreciation by:

  • Leaving a positive online review (Google, Facebook, etc.)
  • Referring them to friends planning similar events
  • Sharing your experience (appropriately) on social media
  • Keeping their contact information for future bookings
Thank You Notes

A quick thank-you message or note means more than you might think:

  • "Thank you for making [name]'s bachelorette party so memorable!"
  • "Your professionalism and energy made our celebration perfect!"
  • "We'll definitely be recommending you to friends!"
Future Bookings

The best compliment? Booking them again or referring them to others. Professional entertainers remember great clients and appreciate repeat business.

Handling Special Situations

When Guests Overstep Boundaries

As the host, you're responsible for managing your guests. If someone crosses a line:

  • Intervene immediately: Don't wait for the performer to handle it alone
  • Pull the guest aside: Quickly but firmly remind them of appropriate behavior
  • Apologize to the performer: A quick "I'm so sorry about that" goes a long way
  • Consider removing the guest: In extreme cases, ask them to leave
Your Responsibility: You hired the entertainment and invited the guests. If your guests make the performer uncomfortable, that reflects on you. Protect the performer and maintain a respectful environment.

If Something Goes Wrong

Issues can arise even with professional entertainers. If there's a problem:

During the Performance
  • Address immediate safety concerns or serious boundary violations
  • For minor issues, let the professional handle it—they're experienced
  • Don't interrupt the flow unless absolutely necessary
After the Performance
  • Discuss concerns politely and privately with the performer
  • If unresolved, contact the booking company afterward
  • Be specific about what was wrong and what you expected
  • Give professionals a chance to make it right
Legitimate Concerns vs. Unrealistic Expectations

Legitimate concerns:

  • Performer arrived significantly late without communication
  • Performance was much shorter than agreed upon
  • Performer was unprofessional or inappropriate
  • Agreed-upon elements were missing

Unrealistic expectations:

  • Expecting services beyond what was discussed
  • Wanting the performance to be different than the performer's style
  • Being unhappy with things outside the performer's control (guests' reactions, etc.)
  • Expecting the performer to stay and socialize after the show

Privacy and Discretion

The Performer's Privacy

Professional entertainers value their privacy:

  • Don't ask for personal phone numbers or social media
  • Don't try to contact them outside of professional channels
  • Respect that their stage name may differ from their legal name
  • Don't share their personal information with others
Your Privacy

Professional entertainers also respect your privacy:

  • They won't share details of your event publicly
  • They won't post identifying photos without permission
  • Your address and personal information remain confidential
  • What happens at your party stays at your party
